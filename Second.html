Fun fact!

Octopuses have three hearts! Two pump blood to the gills, while the third pumps it to the rest of the body. When they swim, the heart that pumps to the body stops, which is why they prefer crawling over swimming.

Why?

Octopuses prefer crawling because it's more efficient for them. When they swim, their main heart stops, making it harder to circulate oxygen and energy throughout their bodies. Crawling lets them keep all three hearts pumping, which helps them conserve energy and function better.
